2021 brings the biggest life change in years. Times two.

## Willow and Finley

The twins (or as we call them, ["The Beans"](https://www.instagram.com/thebeans520)) arrive in June after a 5 week NICU stay. They get their own graduation onesies upon release.

!["The Beans" as we call them got their own graduation onesies upon their release from their 5 week NICU stay](/images/blog/the-beans-have-arrived/PXL_20210622_011015283.MP.jpg)

Once home and free to parent for ourselves without the aid of 24/7 NICU nurse care, we settled into expected chaos with lots of long naps, belying the tumultuous weeks to come. Only a few little worries about what alarms they could have still been setting off were quelled and we did NOT buy in to the Owl active tracking as much as all the paranoia moms on FB literally subscribe to. Overreporting is its own kind of problem that can't be ignored.

For fun I've started tracking every single feed timing and volume, every nap length, every diaper change (and content!) in "Glow" Baby tracker for android. Went ahead and threw $60 for the year of complete tracking options. The goal is to ensure they always nap at the same time to keep us from going crazy. I'll get around to visualizing it all once they let me sleep again.

![Lisa takes the photo while Paisley and Figaro look on in abject wonder](/images/blog/the-beans-have-arrived/PXL_20210705_211557515.MP.jpg)

## Getting Them Started Early

Babies love JavaScript. Or at least they love chewing on programming books and wearing the right outfits.

## Still Showing Up

The workshop gets quieter (or rather, the noise shifts from power tools to crying). Every project timeline expands from "this weekend" to "sometime in the next six months, maybe." But somehow I'm still showing up to standup.

![Somehow still showing up to standup with twins on a green screen.](/images/blog/the-beans-have-arrived/IMG_0423.jpg)

Clearly with different personalities from day one, I'm here for the slog of parenthood again for the first time in a decade, x2... yet somehow it feels 3x harder than with just one? Bless you singleton parents for your worries, but let us both bow in reverence to the 3+ multiple familes. If you know of one please send them some food or just like money or something.

We're doing this life adventure this time around without much of a villiage to aid us, looks like we'll all have to be a bit stronger but I've got high hopes we can make and build a better future for them eventually.
