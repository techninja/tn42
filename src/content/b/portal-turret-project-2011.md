Portal 2 comes out in 2011 and like everyone else, I can't stop thinking about turrets.

## The Collaboration

I meet Michael Curry at Maker Faire KC, an incredible maker who's deep into 3D printing. Later, he reaches out with the idea and sends me a finished printed turret body with servos already in place. He asks if I can give it some personality. My job: make it speak.

![Michael's prototype with camera! I'd have it speaking in no time.](/images/blog/portal-turret-project-2011/2011-08-20 17.13.05.jpg)

I get a Raspberry Pi hooked up to a speaker running the iconic turret voice lines. "I see you." "Are you still there?" "I don't blame you." Motion detection via PIR sensor triggers the audio and wakes the turret from sleep mode. Camera tracking is slow but possible, enough to give the impression it's following you. The combination of Michael's physical build and the audio personality makes it genuinely startling when it activates.

## Assembly

The three "deployable" legs are actually bent square steel rod that has to be carefully inserted into the leg prints and hip sockets. It's fiddly but once they're in, it stands on its own and looks proper menacing.

The print quality on this version isn't quite there, preventing the servo-driven gun ports from opening smoothly. But the audio and tracking work great, which is really the whole point.

![Finally powered on and ready! But the print quality was a bit lacking, preventing servo opening of the gun ports.](/images/blog/portal-turret-project-2011/20150409_174822.jpg)

It lives at that sweet spot where gaming, electronics, and physical building overlap. Which, honestly, is pretty much where I'm happiest making things. The turret becomes a recurring crowd favorite at every event we bring it to. Something about a robot that politely threatens you while guarding snacks just never gets old.
