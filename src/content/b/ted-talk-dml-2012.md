## DML 2012

Where I actually get on stage myself is at DML (Digital Media & Learning) in San Francisco. I'm on a panel with Mark Surman (CEO of Mozilla), Gever Tulley, Sylvia, and Jess Klein. The topic is hands-on digital learning, what works, what doesn't, and how we get more of it into the world.

![On stage at DML 2012 with CEO of Mozilla Mark Surman, Gever Tulley & Jess Klein](/images/blog/ted-talk-dml-2012/MVI_0863.mp4)

Balancing a multi-mic panel is a little fun to learn while on stage but it's not too crazy as long as you're not fanboying too hard. Mozilla is pretty cool though! It's a balance with providing more than just anecdotes, but attempt to parse or provide insight where I can from my perspective. It's all a blur now, but I did hear clapping so I'm going to guess we did a good job!

I've been tinkering with electronics since I was about 7, and I'm a professional web developer for a few years by this point. What's clearly missing in the STEM education world is this kind of "young curious female lead" representation and there seems to be a TON of money and interest to try to bring that to the forefront right now.

Personally I've always been in awe of Carl Sagan and Bill Nye, the way they make the unseen mechanics that govern the universe, better seen and understood to those who often only pay attention to what they seen in front of themselves. What Sylvia brings is a shared thought that it would be fun to show people cool stuff and that it isn't so difficult for a regular kid to do it. Part of the challenge is that media always leans toward "Genius Girl doing everything" when the reality is a regular kid with help, following instructions, and being brave enough to try new things.

The [full DML recap](https://sylviashow.com/blog/techninja/2012/03/15/dml-wrap-make-photoshoot-and-more/) covers more of the conference details.

## The Demo

I also brought a playable demo to the conference floor totally not hastily slapped together last minute! Squishy Space Race. It's a terrible game you can still have fun with as you yell at play dough, a TV screen, and your fellow players for a solid 10 seconds.

![Demo uses an arduino with the ethernet module, acting as a data server where the web app reads analog inputs from the 3 player inputs ~60hz, play dough on copper pads and a fire button for shooting 3D WebGL asteroids.](/images/blog/ted-talk-dml-2012/IMG_0893.jpg)

An Arduino with the ethernet module acts as a data server reading analog inputs from three player stations. Each one is play dough on copper pads with a fire button for shooting asteroids. It's ridiculous, tactile, and exactly the kind of thing that gets people to stop and ask questions. Oh and I put some very loud sounds to drown out the din of a few hundred people talking at once.

![Squishy space race is a terrible game you can still have fun with as you yell at play dough.](/images/blog/ted-talk-dml-2012/IMG_0894.jpg)

These conferences are exhausting but energizing. Every event is a reminder that this thing we started in a living room with a webcam is landing with people in ways I never expected. Now if only I could figure out a way to do this without living out of a suitcase.
